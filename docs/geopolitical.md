### Terra Invicta 1.0 Data Block: Geopolitical Layer

**1. National Relations & Unification**

* **Diplomatic Status:** Nations are **Independent**, **Allied**, **Federated**, or **Rivals**.
* **Managing Relations:** Use the "Relations" tab. Moving to "Ally" costs **10 Influence** and triggers a 1-month cooldown.
* **Federation:** A prerequisite for peaceful unification. All members share a defensive pact and a combined "Mission Control" pool.
* **Unification (Absorption):**
* **Prerequisite:** Absorbing nation must have a **Claim** on the target's capital.
* **Government Match:** Democracy scores must be within **1.5 points** for peaceful merges.
* **Procedure:** Done via "Set National Policy" mission. In 1.0, territorial transfers are processed region-by-region.


* **Regional Hegemony:** Large federations (e.g., EU, Pan-Asian Combine) receive **Investment Point (IP) bonuses** based on the total number of unified regions, incentivising expansion.

**2. Control Points (CP)**

* **CP Count:** Scales with national GDP (1 to 6 points).
* **CP Cost:** Determined by the nation's monthly IP before deductions. Large economies like 1.0 China/USA are significantly more "expensive" to hold.
* **Gaining CP:**
* **Control Point Mission:** Basic takeover.
* **Crackdown:** Disables a CP and removes its benefits; provides a massive bonus to Purge.
* **Purge:** Steals a CP directly. Success is near-guaranteed on "Cracked" points.
* **Coup d'Etat:** Resets all CPs. Success scales with national **Unrest**.


* **Losing CP:**
* **Overcap Penalty:** Being over your total CP Cap grants enemies a **+1.0 bonus** to Crackdown/Purge for every point over the limit.
* **Abandonment:** Voluntary removal leaves the point "Cracked" for 180 days.



**3. Executive Control Point (Right-most Slot)**

* **Entry Rule:** Standard missions require holding at least one other CP in that nation first (unless it is the only CP).
* **Powers:** Only the Executive owner can change **National Priorities**, declare **War**, manage **Alliances**, or use **Set National Policy**.
* **Consolidation Cooldown:** After gaining the Executive CP, a **180-day (6 month)** cooldown applies before you can perform "Set National Policy" (Federating, Unifying, or changing Alliances).
* **Coup Exception:** A successful Coup allows the victor to take the Executive CP immediately with **no consolidation cooldown**.

**4. Earth Resource Generation (Investment Priorities)**
| Priority | Primary Resource/Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Economy** | Increases GDP/Capital. Can develop regions into **Core Economic Regions (CER)** ($500bn GDP threshold) which act like permanent "Oil" bonuses. |
| **Welfare** | Reduces Inequality; removes **Colony Malus** (IP penalty) from non-core regions. |
| **Knowledge** | Generates **Research**; increases Democracy score. |
| **Unity** | Generates **Influence**; acts as a passive **Public Campaign** mission to shift opinion. |
| **Military** | Lowers **Unrest**; increases Miltech level. |
| **Spoils** | Generates **Money**; increases Inequality and Corruption. Most efficient in nations with "Extractive" sectors. |
| **Boost** | Generates **Boost** (Tons to orbit/month) based on launch site latitude. |
| **Mission Control** | Increases **MC Cap** to support space assets. Scaling is harder in 1.0 compared to EA. |

**5. Defensive & Stability Mechanics**

* **Defend Interests:** Councilor mission adding a **+10 modifier** to CP defense. In 1.0, high Investigation (25+) is required to reliably bypass this via Crackdown.
* **Resting Cohesion:** High GDP per capita (approx. $1,000 = 0.1 reduction) naturally lowers the "resting" unrest level of a nation.
* **Public Opinion:** High popularity acts as a multiplier for all Councilor missions within that nation's borders.
* **Enthrall Public:** An alien-specific subversion mission that ignores standard CP rules; defense scales with a nation's **Science** output and total population.
